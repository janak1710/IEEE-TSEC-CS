ABOUT US

IEEE Computer society is the world’s leading organization of computer professionals. Founded in 1946, it is the largest of the 39 societies of the IEEE. The IEEE Computer Society’s vision is to be the leading provider of technical information, community services and personalized services to the world’s computing professionals.

The Student Branch at TSEC (IEEE-TSEC) is particularly renowned for its efforts to make available, the latest technologies to its members. IEEE-TSEC Computer Society, at the outset established itself amongst the students, as an endeavor to make its members proficient at their computer skills. It has been actively involved in satisfying the technical inclinations of all of its members who swear by its activities and benefits. Computer Society prides itself on its ability to tap the vast sea of talent within its members, preparing them for the professional world.

EVENTS FOR MOSAIC 2010

  • Conference on Ethical Hacking

    A Conference is an event where a group of people come together to deliberate on a particular topic. With a topic like Ethical Hacking and professionals hailing from a firm like the Institute of Information Security, the conference on 22nd and 23rd February was surely going to be interesting. Just as expected, students thronged the Seminar Hall for the event on both days. Mr. Nikhil Wagholikar from Institute of Information Security conducted the event for both days. He shared his extensive knowledge on Computer Security with the students and also entertained any doubts that the students had, making it an interesting and interactive conference. IEEE-TSEC Computer Society received a positive feedback from the students as well as from Mr. Wagholikar at the end of the event.

  • Workshop on MAYA (Animation)

    MAYA, the king of all animation softwares, sparks interest in students at the mere mention of its name. Again, as expected, lab no. 405, the venue of the workshop went full on 24th February, 2010, the day of the event. The workshop was conducted by Maximus, a reputed animation firm all over India. To get the students acquainted with the basics of the software, they were asked to animate a lamp. And such was the quality of the workshop that all the students present were able to create a perfect hopping lamp. Naturally, the feedback from the students as well as the firm was positive.

  • Workshop on Web Designing

    The next event lined up was a workshop on Web Designing, to be conducted by a member of IEEE-TSEC Computer Society, on 2nd of March, 2010. The event was scheduled keeping in mind the prerequisites for the Annual Summer Placements of IEEE-TSEC Computer Society. Like the earlier events, Web Designing also received a great response from the students. The students got a hands on experience in creating a web page. As always, we received the best of feedbacks from the students.

  • Seminar on Education Beyond Engineering

    The finale of MOSAIC 2010 was the Seminar by KIC Education on 9th of March, 2010. The event was scheduled considering the enormous decision that budding engineers have to face – What to do after we are engineers? The Seminar cleared the doubts that the attendees had, focusing on both career choices – MS and MBA. Students were given an idea of how to prepare for examinations like GRE, GMAT and TOEFL. All in all, the students left the Seminar Hall visibly relaxed and confident of their path after engineering.



EVENTS FOR IGNITION 2010

  • Workshop on Adobe PHOTOSHOP :

    Photoshop was again introduced to the students on the request of our members. As expected, the workshop garnered a crowd of 92 students – a record for any other event in our college. The workshop was conducted by senior members of IEEE-TSEC Computer Society who were also members of the creative team of IEEE-TSEC. Having designed numerous posters themselves, the team carefully explained each and every intricate detail of the software and the students were only too happy to give us a positive feedback.

  • HustlePlay :

    IGNITION 2010 introduced a brand new concept for its members in the form of Hustleplay, a series of contests that were challenging, yet served as a reprieve from the daily dose of lectures in college. Hustleplay was comprised of a technical quiz, decoding anagrams, solving riddles, a typing contest and coding in the end. The entire event was well organized by the committee – the reward being groundbreaking participation by the Society members.
